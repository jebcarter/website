About Me

Hello! Thanks for visiting my page! My name is Jeb Carter, and I am a sophomore studying Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Princeton University. I am pursuing minors in Applications of Computing and Robotics & Intelligent Systems. As a student at Princeton, I am a Peer Academic Advisor as well as Tour Manager and Social Media Chair for my a cappella group, the Princeton Roaring 20. I also sing tenor with the Princeton Chapel Choir and I play on the club tennis team.

In addition to my singing extracurriculars, I am also a piano and guitar player. Needless to say, music is a really important part of my life! Other passions of mine include the Oxford Comma, for which I am a fervent advocate, and Comic Sans, for which I am a fervent opponent. I love a good pun and I am a huge Atlanta United fan! (Go ATL!)

Engineering Background

Since high school, engineering has been a passion and a dream of mine. I found physics and math to be particularly exciting subjects, so I came to college knowing that I wanted to apply my technical knowledge to solving real-world problems. As a result, I've had the opportunity to work on several engineering projects as an undergraduate at Princeton. These projects have been enjoyable, rewarding, and educational, and this website is an opportunity to showcase some of the most exciting work that I've gotten to do in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering to date!
