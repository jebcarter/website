About Me

Hello! Thanks for visiting my page! My name is Jeb Carter, and I am a sophomore studying Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at Princeton University. I am pursuing minors in Applications of Computing and Robotics & Intelligent Systems. As a student at Princeton, I am a Peer Academic Advisor as well as Tour Manager and Social Media Chair for my a cappella group, the Princeton Roaring 20. I also sing tenor with the Princeton Chapel Choir and I play on the club tennis team.

In addition to singing, I am also a piano and guitar player. Other passions of mine include the Oxford Comma, for which I am a fervent advocate, and Comic Sans, for which I am a fervent opponent. I love a good pun and I am a huge Atlanta United fan! (Go ATL!)

Engineering Background

Since high school, engineering has been a passion and a dream of mine, and I've always found physics and math to be particularly exciting subjects. As a result, I've had the opportunity to work on several engineering projects as an undergraduate at Princeton. These projects have been incredibly rewarding and educational, and this website is an opportunity to showcase some of the most exciting work that I've gotten to do in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering to date!

Computer Science Background

Computer Science has been one of the most recent and exciting additions to my academic interests. Two years ago, I didn't know an if-statement from a while-loop, and I definitely didn't have the ability to create large-scale, start-to-finish computational projects in mutliple languages. My freshman year, I took an intro computer science class and I haven't stopped since. Since then, it has become a true passion of mine, one that I am always eager to incorporate into every project that I work on. In fact, it was my interest in computer science that motivated me to create this website in HTML and CSS rather than use a template from a website builder. I love the experience of building a project from the ground up and watching it come together as I work. It's what draws me to engineering and it's what excites me so much about computer science. Similarly to my engineering work, this website is an opportunity to highlight some of my most interesting and exciting computer science work that I have done to date.
