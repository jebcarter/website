M U S I C

Musical Work

Music is incredibly important to me. I play guitar & piano and I sing with two groups at Princeton, Roaring 20 and the Princeton Chapel Choir. The first of these groups, Roaring 20, is Princeton's premier co-ed a cappella group. The second, Chapel Choir, is a group of around 80 singers who perform for the Princeton Chapel's non-denominational worship service every Sunday on campus.

Roaring 20

Roaring 20, for which I sing tenor, has 15-20 members; we sing a diverse repertoire from jazz pieces to classic oldies to today's pop. I've served as President, Tour Manager, and Social Media Chair for R20. At various stages in my Princeton career, I have been responsible for leading the group, acting as its public representative, organizing and booking travel, housing, food, events, and performances for 15-20 singers on both domestic and international tours. I also managed all social media accounts for the group. In addition to handling several organizational- and publicity-related tasks, I also write musical arrangements for the group. Arrangements of mine include Midnight Train to Georgia, Please Please Me, and Karma Chameleon.

We sing dozens of performances each year, ranging from arch sings to concerts to professional gigs in cities across the world. See one of our more recent music video, a mash-up of three Justin Timberlake songs (below).

Also check us out on our website, Facebook, Spotify, Youtube, and Instagram (@princetonroaring20).

Chapel Choir

The Chapel Choir is a wonderful group of musicians and a close-knit, albeit large, family. We sing for the non-denominational church service in the beautiful Princeton Chapel every Sunday morning as well as two concerts every year, one in the fall and one in the spring. Recent concerts have included singing pieces by Mozart, Bach, and Brahms. We've had the privelege of being recorded professionally for a piece in a holiday Christmas album and we boast the largest rubber duck collection in all of New Jersey! Singing with the Chapel Choir is an absolute joy, and one of my favorite activities on campus. I'm also fortunate that Chapel Choir is an on-campus job. As such, all singers are paid an hourly wage for both rehearsals and sunday services as employees of the Office of Religious Life.

The video below was recorded during April of 2020, during COVID-19 quarantine. Since the choir could not be together to perform for the yearly Easter service, the group came together to record ourselves singing our respective parts to create an Easter medley from around the world! The video includes over 50 participants and speaks volumes to the wonderful community and music of the Chapel Choir. The piece is "I Got Me Flowers" by Daniel Burton.
